History

Artistic power struggles.

Early days

A German craftsman named Johannes Gutenberg developed a way to automate book copying via a press. This process utilizes an inked top and a lower platen below which in turn. This helped produce books quickly and accurately that could produce 250 pages.

First editorial

Dates back in 1754, a cartoon by Benjamin Franklin is one of the first well known illustration in a newspaper cartoon. Entitled as "Join or die", it depicts a snake divided in eight pieces which represents the eight colonies. This often appears in every conflict through the revolutionary war at that time. These illustrations have known to be a dangerous game

Throughout history, these illustrators risk reprisal as they often poke fun or critique the status quo. This what happened with the 19th century french lithographer Honore Daumer which he portrays the king as a grotesque giant that is exploiting the hard work of his people. The king fined and imprisoned the artist then banned his cartoons.
